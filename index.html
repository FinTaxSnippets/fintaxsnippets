FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common tax questions.

What is the last date to file ITR for FY 2025-26?
+
The due date for ITR filing for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) for individuals not liable to audit is July 31, 2026. Late filing is possible up to December 31, 2026 with a late fee of ₹1,000 to ₹5,000.
Which ITR form should I file if I am a salaried employee?
+
If your total income is below ₹50 Lakh with only salary, one house property and interest income — file ITR-1 (Sahaj). Capital gains or multiple properties — ITR-2. Use our ITR Form Selector tool above to find out instantly.
Is the New Tax Regime better than the Old Regime?
+
It depends on your deductions. New Regime offers ₹12 Lakh zero tax (FY 2025-26) but no deductions like 80C, HRA, 80D. If your total deductions exceed ₹3–3.5 Lakh, Old Regime may save more. Use our Income Tax Calculator to compare both regimes.
What documents do I need for ITR filing?
+
Key documents: Form 16 (from employer), AIS / Form 26AS (from IT portal), bank statements, investment proofs (80C, 80D), home loan certificate (if applicable), and capital gains statements from broker (if applicable).
What is Form 26AS and why is it important?
+
Form 26AS is your Tax Credit Statement — it shows all TDS deducted, advance tax paid and income reported against your PAN. Any mismatch with your ITR can lead to a notice from the Income Tax Department.
Can I file ITR if I missed the July 31 deadline?
+
Yes — you can file a belated return up to December 31 with a late fee of ₹1,000 (income up to ₹5 Lakh) or ₹5,000 (income above ₹5 Lakh). However, you cannot carry forward capital losses in a belated return.
